\begin{problem}
  Let $f(x) = (x-3)^{-2}$.
  Show that there is no value $c$ in $(1,4)$ such that $f(4) - f(1) = f^{\prime}(c) (4-1)$.
  Why does this not contradict the Mean Value Theorem?
\end{problem}
